UX writing guide

Words are the interface

Vallejo copy reads like an institution that knows its platform. Every label, message, and term is professional, straightforward, confident, and helpful — the triad of Precision, Institutional Confidence, and Vitality made legible. The fastest tell of a machine-written screen is the writing; this guide is how we avoid it.

One source of truth

Generate all member-facing copy through these rules. Layer 1 (the why) is the Copart Design Vault; Vallejo is Layer 2 (the how). When they conflict, the Vault wins.

Brand voice

Voice is constant; tone adapts to context. The voice is professional, straightforward, confident, and helpful. Each trait maps to the triad: Professional and Straightforward serve Precision, Confident is Institutional Confidence, and Helpful is Vitality.

Good — professional

Respect the member's time and money. No fluff, no filler.

Your bid of $4,500 has been placed.

Avoid — over-eager

Excitement is not warmth. Padding wastes the member's attention.

Awesome! You just placed a bid!

Good — straightforward

Say exactly what is happening. No jargon the member did not ask for.

This lot has a clean title.

Avoid — jargon

Internal language is not a member's vocabulary.

Documentation status is clear.

Good — confident

We know our platform. State facts; do not hedge.

Bidding closes at 2:30 PM EST.

Avoid — hedging

"Should", "might", and "probably" erode trust.

Should close around 2:30 or so.

Good — helpful

Every message moves the member toward their goal.

Enter your VIN to check eligibility.

Avoid — terse

A requirement without a next step leaves the member stuck.

VIN required.

The three hard prohibitions

Institutional Confidence draws three lines and never crosses them.

Good — no hedging

Bidding closes in 2 minutes. Place your bid now.

Avoid — hedged

You might want to try to place a bid soon, we think.

Good — no over-explaining

Saved to your watchlist.

Avoid — padded

We have gone ahead and saved this lot to your watchlist so you can find it later.

Good — no exclamation marks

Bid placed successfully.

Avoid — exclaiming

Bid placed!

Tone

The voice never changes — the tone shifts to meet the moment. Informational copy is neutral, encouraging copy is warm, urgent copy is direct, and error copy is calm and solution-focused. Never use humor, sarcasm, or playfulness in an error state.

Good — informational

Neutral and factual. State the fact and stop.

This lot is located in Dallas, TX.

Good — encouraging

Warm and supportive, never giddy.

You're all set — your watchlist will notify you when bidding opens.

Good — urgent

Direct and clear. Urgency is in the timing, not in punctuation.

Bidding closes in 2 minutes. Place your bid now.

Avoid — humor in errors

A frustrated member meets clarity, never cleverness.

Yikes — looks like our hamsters stopped running.

Sentence case — everywhere

Capitalize the first word and proper nouns only. This applies to page headings, button labels, nav items, tab labels, table headers, menu items, and badges. The only exceptions are proper nouns (Copart, CrashedToys), acronyms (VIN, NMVTIS), and product names used as proper nouns.

Good — sentence case

My saved vehicles

Find vehicles
Avoid — Title Case

My Saved Vehicles

Find Vehicles

Punctuation

No periods on buttons, labels, table headers, nav items, or badges; periods on full sentences. Serial comma in every list. Em dash joins clauses; en dash spans ranges. No exclamation marks anywhere; question marks only in genuine questions.

Good — serial comma

Year, make, and model.

Avoid — missing comma

Year, make and model.

Good — em dash joins clauses

Payment failed — update your payment method.

Avoid — hyphen splice

Payment failed - update your payment method.

Good — en dash for ranges

$1,200–$3,500 · Mon–Fri

Avoid — "to" or hyphen ranges

$1,200 to $3,500 · Mon-Fri

Good — no period on a label
Clean title
Avoid — period on a label
Clean title.

Active voice

Active voice, always. Name the actor and the action. Copart does things ("We saved your bid"); the member does things ("You placed a bid"). Passive constructions read as evasive and slow.

Good — active

We saved your bid.

Avoid — passive

Your bid has been saved.

Good — active

We'll notify you when bidding opens.

Avoid — passive

You will be notified when bidding is opened.

Tense

Present for current state, past for a completed action, imperative for instructions, and future for upcoming events. Match the tense to what the member is actually experiencing.

Good — present for state

3 items in your watchlist.

Good — past for completion

Bid placed successfully.

Good — imperative for instruction

Enter your VIN.

Good — future for upcoming

Bidding opens tomorrow at 10:00 AM ET.

Person and pronouns

Second person ("you", "your") addresses the member — never "the user". First person plural ("we") is Copart. Use "they" for an unknown member. Contractions are welcome (don't, you'll, we're); regional contractions are not (ain't, y'all, would've).

Good — second person

Your watchlist will notify you when bidding opens.

Avoid — "the user"

The user's watchlist will notify the user.

Good — standard contractions

You'll get a notification, and we're tracking the lot for you.

Avoid — regional contractions

Y'all would've gotten a notification.

Numbers, dates, time, and more

Spell out 1–9 in prose; use numerals for 10+ and always for prices, bids, lot numbers, and counts. Currency drops cents over $100. Dates abbreviate the month with no leading zeros. Times use a 12-hour clock with a full timezone. Handle 0, 1, and many. Truncate only as a last resort.

Numbers

Good — spell out 1–9 in prose

You saved three vehicles this week.

Avoid — numeral for small counts in prose

You saved 3 vehicles this week.

Currency, dates, and time

Good — no cents over $100

Final bid $47,250

Avoid — trailing zero cents

Final bid $47,250.00

Good — abbreviated month, no leading zero

Sale date Feb 22, 2026

Avoid — numeric date with zeros

Sale date 02/22/2026

Good — 12-hour clock, full timezone

Closes at 2:30 PM EST

Avoid — 24-hour, no timezone

Closes at 14:30

Pluralization

Handle 0, 1, and many. Never show "0 results" — use zero-state phrasing instead.

Good — 0 / 1 / many

No results

1 result

24 results

Avoid — naive plural

0 results

1 results

24 result(s)

Truncation

If copy exceeds its budget, rewrite — don't truncate. Truncation is a last resort for dynamic content only: ellipsis after the last full word, minimum 3 visible words, full text in a tooltip. Null table cells use an em dash, never "N/A" or a blank.

Good — ellipsis after a full word

2019 Toyota Camry SE — front-end collision…

Avoid — mid-word cut

2019 Toyota Camry SE — front-end colli...

Microcopy

The small strings carry the system. Each component has a copy contract: a button is a verb, an error is "[what happened] — [how to fix]", a navigation label is a noun. Examples below pair the rule with a live Vallejo component wherever the component ships.

Button labels

Start with a verb, be specific, and keep prices and amounts out of the label. Show the amount in adjacent text — buttons are for actions only.

Good — verb-first, amount adjacent

Bid amount $4,500

Avoid — vague label, price inside
Good — destructive names the object
Avoid — bare "Delete"

Error messages

Canonical format: [what happened] — [how to fix]. Calm, solution-focused, never blaming the member. Inline field errors name the valid format; banner errors lead with what happened, then what to do.

Good — banner: what happened, then fix

Payment failed — update your payment method to continue.

Avoid — vague and blaming

Oops! Something went wrong. You entered an invalid card.

Good — inline field error names the format

Enter a valid lot number (8 digits).

Avoid — "Invalid input"

Invalid input.

Empty states

Heading names what's missing, body explains how to fill it, and a CTA resolves it. Never "No data" or "Nothing to show".

Good — heading, body, CTA

No saved lots yet

Search for vehicles and save them to your watchlist to see them here.

Avoid — dead end

No data

Your list is empty.

Confirmations

Confirm the specific action with its real values — the lot and the amount — not a generic "Success".

Good — specific and complete

Your bid of $4,500 on Lot 78901234 has been placed.

Avoid — generic and loud

Success! Action completed.

Field labels

Labels name the field — they are not instructions and never become the placeholder. Optional fields read "(Optional)"; never a required star.

Good — names the field, optional suffix
Avoid — instruction as label, required star

Placeholder text

Placeholders show an example format only. They never repeat the label and are never a substitute for it.

Good — example format
Avoid — repeats the label or "Type here"

Modal actions

Title is the topic; body carries what's needed to decide and any amount. The primary button is the specific action — never "OK" or "Yes" — and the secondary is "Cancel".

Good — specific primary, amount in body

Confirm your bid

You're about to bid $4,500 on Lot 78901234. This can't be undone.

Avoid — OK / Yes, price in button

Are you sure?

Confirm this action.

Toast messages

One past-tense sentence confirming the action just taken, under 60 characters. Offer an undo where the action is reversible.

Good — past tense, one line, undo

Lot removed from watchlist. Undo

Avoid — exclaiming, off-glossary

Success! Item added to your favorites list!

Navigation labels

Top-level navigation labels are nouns, never verbs. Members click to go somewhere, not to do something. Breadcrumbs match the page title exactly.

Good — noun labels
Vehicles My bids Dashboard
Avoid — verb labels
Find vehicles View my bids Go to dashboard

Status communication

State the fact with its value, calmly. Status is never signaled by color alone — every chip carries a word, and LIVE keeps its dot. Reserve urgency for the timing, not the punctuation.

Good — fact + value, word-bearing chip
Live

Bidding closes Feb 22 at 2:30 PM EST.

Lot sold — final bid $12,400.

Avoid — vague urgency, exclaiming
Closing!

Auction ending soon!

This item has been sold.

Terminology glossary

Use these terms consistently; never alternate between synonyms within the same flow or page. If a term isn't here, choose the simplest, most common word and use it everywhere. Mono (JetBrains Mono) is reserved for the figures members compare — VIN, lot number, bid, and timestamp.

No emoji — ever

No emoji in any UI copy. Use the Iconic icon set only — inline SVG with stroke="currentColor" and fill="none". Emoji and icon overuse are flagged AI-generated anti-patterns.
