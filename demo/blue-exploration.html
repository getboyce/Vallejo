Per-Stop Evaluation
Each stop evaluated individually. Verdict based on perceptual RGB delta, role in the ramp, and adjacency to the new 300/400 stops.
Blue 50 #F6F9FD 214° → 210° Keep
RGB delta: 1.0 — literally one pixel of difference. At 98% lightness, hue is imperceptible. This color is doing its job as a barely-there page tint. Changing it would be change for change's sake.
Blue 100 #EEF3FB 223° → 216° Nudge
RGB delta: 3.0 — subtle, but this is the biggest hue outlier in the ramp at 223°. That's 13° off from the new 300. At 96% lightness the purple pull is barely visible in isolation, but in a ramp strip it's the warmest stop. Nudge to 216° — splits the difference between the whisper-lights and the new mids. Proposed:
Blue 200 #E1ECF9 221° → 214° Nudge
RGB delta: 4.0 — still subtle, but this is the critical bridge stop. It sits right next to the new 300 (210°), and the 11° hue jump from 221° → 210° is the one visible seam in the ramp. Nudge to 214° — halves the gap to 300 without meaningfully changing how it reads in its current role (selected rows, info fills). Proposed:
Blue 300 210° New — Locked
Your chosen value. Clean true-blue section background. 210° at 84% lightness reads unmistakably blue with zero purple drift.
Blue 400 210° New — Locked
Your chosen value. Mid-tone accent blue. 210° at 70% lightness — confident blue that never competes with the 600 CTA.
Blue 600 #2662D9 220° Brand Anchor — Locked
The brand anchor. 220° at 50% lightness reads as a clean, confident blue because at high saturation + mid lightness the hue is fully expressed. Untouchable.
Blue 800 #063598 221° Keep
RGB delta to 210° would be 27.0 — a massive, visible shift. But the current 221° at 92% saturation and 31% lightness reads as a deep, authoritative blue, not purple. It pairs perfectly with 600 (only 1° apart). Changing it to 210° would actually push it toward teal. Leave it.
Blue 900 #0F2757 220° Keep
At 220° it shares the 600's hue exactly — this is your dark brand blue. At 20% lightness, hue shift toward purple is invisible. It reads as a commanding dark blue. No reason to touch it.
Final Proposed Ramp
Green = keep, orange = adjusted. The hue naturally warms from 210° in the mids to 220° in the darks — this is perceptually correct. Lighter colors need to lean cooler to read as "blue," darker colors can absorb more hue depth.
